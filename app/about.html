J.P. is CEO and Founder, guiding the strategic direction of the firm and contributing to the global conversation on talent and leadership. He has coached more than 120 executives (half of them CEOs) across every major industry and continues to partner with selected clientele facing critical leadership and human capital challenges.  He is a sought after speaker on the topics of talent, team building, and human potential and regularly addresses both private equity and CEO audiences.  He is also a guest lecturer on ‘Hiring Top Talent’ at the Stanford Graduate School of Business.

Known for his unusual ability to take leaders to new levels of performance, J.P. is an influencer and go-to voice on leadership and executive team effectiveness. He has coached CEOs and boards on issues related to leadership effectiveness, organizational design, succession planning, on-boarding, M&A integration, and conflict intervention. His clients have included Bain Capital, Berkshire Partners, The Carlyle Group, General Atlantic, Madison Dearborn Partners, and more. J.P.’s work and insights have been featured in the Harvard Business Review, Washington Post, New York Times and numerous other publications.

J.P. earned an MBA from Harvard Business School and a B.S. in finance and management with a minor in psychology from the University of Virginia.  He is a CTI-certified executive coach and is licensed by the International Coaching Federation.

“Most CEOs have never truly learned how to assess talent, ask the most powerful leadership questions and proactively shape their own identity. In fact, they often don’t realize that asking for help is one of the most powerful things a leader can do. As executive coaches, it’s a thrill to help supercharge the best and brightest leaders in the world.”

Camille is a Partner charged with both firm leadership and client services responsibilities. With more than a decade of experience as a strategic advisor to executives and teams, she understands what makes organizations and leaders successful.

Camille advises private equity and corporate clients on a range of mission-critical topics, including individual and organizational assessment, leadership development, team effectiveness and coaching. Her blend of empathy and deep analytics gets to the heart of issues and contributes to lasting change for her clients.

Before joining Green Peak, Camille was a top performer with The Boston Consulting Group and served as a Vice President at Lehman Brothers where she advised leaders of governments, municipalities, and non-profit health systems.

Camille earned her MBA from Harvard Business School as well as a B.A. from Columbia College with honors in Economics. She lives in Boston, Massachusetts with her husband, Akhill, and their daughter.

“People have always been the most interesting puzzles for me. By figuring out what makes them tick, where they shine and fail, and how they work together, I help leaders optimize their talent, teams, and organizations.”
